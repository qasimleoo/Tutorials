

SQL JOINs

A JOIN clause is used to combine data from two or more tables based on related columns between them.

How much amount was paid by customer "Ali", what was mode of payment and payment data.


Types: Inner, Left Right, Full (in order of usage but may vary from dev to dev)

Inner: Common between two (intersection)


SELECT column_name(s)  
FROM tableA
INNER JOIN tableB
ON tableA.col_name=tableB.col_name; (value should be same in both tables' columns)

SELECT u.id, a.user_id, a.api_key_value 
FROM users as u
INNER JOIN api_key as a
ON u.id = a.user_id;


LEFT Join:
everything of left and common between both


SELECT column_name(s)
FROM tableA as a
LEFT JOIN tableB as b
ON a.column_name = b.column_name


Right Join:
everything of right and common between both


Full JOIN:
returns all records

SELECT * 
FROM tableA
FULL OUTER JOIN tableB
ON tableA.col_name = tableB.col_name


wfdb=# select u.username, u.id, a.api_key_value from users u join api_key a on a.user_id = u.id where u.username like 'qasim%';
